Research Overview

My research focuses on the design and analysis of novel data-driven first-order methods to support robust and efficient decision making under uncertainty. I am particularly motivated by discovering problem structures pertaining to algorithmic development. Utilizing these structures, my research develops efficient, scalable, and broadly applicable methods with optimal computational and statistical performances, featuring real-life applications in various domains.

1. Policy optimization in reinforcement learning: My research designs efficient policy gradient methods for solving Markov decision processes (MDPs)/reinforcement learning (RL). Methods from my research exhibit the first policy convergence; handle large state spaces; and optimally explore the action space.

2. Robust reinforcement learning: My research addresses robust MDPs/RL from both modeling and methodology aspects. In particular, joining these two aspects produces the first globally convergent method for large-scale robust MDPs/RL, thereby resolving a decade-long open question.

3. Minimax optimization: I study the basic question of designing optimal first-order methods for convex-concave problems, and their natural extensions to structured non-convex non-concave problems with global convergence.

4. Optimization for machine learning: Part of my research aims to understand first-order methods used in practice, and subsequently develops improved methods for training machine learning models.

5. Applications: Applications of aforementioned methodologies in real-life scenarios, including treatment planning in healthcare, multi-agent RL, and recommendation systems in E-commerce.
