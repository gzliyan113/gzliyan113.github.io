Research Interests

I am primarily interested in developing eﬃcient and robust ﬁrst-order methods for data-driven decision making. Part of my research also goes into understanding practical ﬁrst-order methods in modern ML applications.

With above focuses in mind, most of my technical developments concern with the following two areas.

  • Reinforcement Learning: develop policy optimization methods that are algorithmically simple, computationally eﬃcient, and being robust against model uncertainties.
  • Algorithmic Regularization: study the implicit regularization properties aroused purely from the optimization methods in modern ML practices.

I’ve also spent some time interning at industry research labs, improving the training of ultra-large recommendation systems.
